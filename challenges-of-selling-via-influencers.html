Challenges of selling products via influencers

Introduction

Influencer marketing is a form of the social media marketing that involves partnering with individuals who have a strong online presence, known as influencers, to promote products or services.

These influencers have built a following of engaged and loyal fans, and by leveraging their audience, businesses can reach new consumers and increase brand awareness.

In today's digital age, influencer marketing has become an essential tool for businesses looking to connect with consumers. A study by Business Insider Intelligence found that influencer marketing is set to become a $10 billion dollar industry by 2023. This is not surprising, given that 92% of consumers trust recommendations from individuals over brands. Furthermore, influencer marketing campaigns have been shown to be more effective than traditional forms of advertising, with businesses earning an average of $6.50 for every $1 spent on influencer marketing.

Examples of famous figures and brands that have successfully leveraged influencer marketing include Kim Kardashian and Calvin Klein, who used the reality star's massive social media following to promote their underwear line. Another example is the brand Glossier, which has built a cult following by partnering with beauty influencers and leveraging user-generated content to showcase its products.

These examples demonstrate the power of influencer marketing and how it can be used to reach new audiences and drive sales. However, as with any marketing strategy, influencer marketing also presents its own set of challenges. As CPG founders, it's crucial to understand these challenges and develop a strategy that maximizes the return on investment.

Challenges of identifying the right influencer

One of the biggest challenges faced by CPG founders when it comes to influencer marketing is identifying the right influencer. With the rise of influencer marketing, the number of individuals claiming to be influencers has grown exponentially, making it difficult for businesses to separate the wheat from the chaff.

One of the first challenges is identifying influencers with a relevant audience. It's important to choose an influencer whose audience aligns with the target market of the product. For example, if a business is selling a line of organic skincare products, it would be more effective to partner with an influencer who has a following of individuals interested in natural and organic beauty products rather than an influencer who primarily focuses on fashion. A study by Bloglovin' found that 70% of millennial consumers are more likely to make a purchase based on a social media referral.

Another challenge is measuring the influence of an influencer. While an influencer may have a large following, it doesn't necessarily mean that they have a high level of engagement or that their audience is truly interested in the product or service being promoted. Metrics such as engagement rate (likes, comments, shares) and click-through rate can provide a more accurate measure of an influencer's influence.

Lastly, ensuring the authenticity of the influencer's audience is also a big challenge. With the rise of fake followers and bots, it's important to verify that an influencer's audience is genuine and not artificially inflated. Many influencer marketing platforms now offer tools to check the authenticity of an influencer's audience.

CPG founders have to be extra careful while identifying the right influencer, as choosing the wrong influencer can result in a significant loss of time and money. A study by Influencer.co found that 63% of marketers have worked with fake influencers at least once.

Examples of famous figures and brands that have successfully leveraged influencer marketing include the brand Glossier, which has built a cult following by partnering with beauty influencers and leveraging user-generated content to showcase its products. Another example is Mercedes-Benz, which partnered with fashion influencer Danielle Bernstein to promote its latest luxury car, the S-Class. Mercedes-Benz was able to reach a new and relevant audience by partnering with Danielle, whose followers are primarily interested in fashion and luxury goods.

As CPG founders, it's important to remember that finding the right influencer is key to the success of the campaign. By taking the time to find influencers with a relevant audience, measuring their influence, and ensuring the authenticity of their audience, CPG founders can maximize the return on investment for their influencer marketing campaigns.

Challenges of creating effective campaigns

A major hurdle that CPG founders must overcome in their influencer marketing efforts is aligning campaign goals with the influencer's personal brand. This is especially true for influencers who have a strong personal brand, such as celebrity chefs or fitness gurus. For example, if you are a CPG founder of a plant-based protein powder and want to partner with a celebrity chef known for their meat-centric recipes, it may be difficult to align your campaign goals with their personal brand. This can lead to confusion for the influencer's audience and ultimately, a lack of engagement with your product.

To overcome this challenge, CPG founders should research the influencer's personal brand and audience before reaching out to them. This will help you identify which influencers are a good fit for your campaign and will be more likely to align their personal brand with your goals.

Additionally, CPG founders can work with the influencer to create content that is authentic to their personal brand while still promoting your product. This can be done by highlighting the features of your product that align with the influencer's personal brand and values.

Another challenge CPG founders face is creating content that resonates with the influencer's audience. It's important to remember that the influencer's audience is not your target market, so it is crucial to understand the influencer's audience and create content that appeals to them. For example, if you are a CPG founder of a skincare brand and want to partner with a beauty influencer with a young adult following, you'll need to create content that resonates with this audience, such as makeup tutorials or skincare tips for acne-prone skin.

To create content that resonates with the influencer's audience, CPG founders should research the influencer's audience and identify their interests, needs, and pain points. This can be done by analyzing the influencer's social media metrics and studying their engagement with their followers. Additionally, CPG founders can work with the influencer to create content that aligns with their audience's interests and needs. This can be done by incorporating the influencer's personal brand and values into the content and highlighting the features of your product that align with their audience's needs.

Finally, CPG founders face the challenge of measuring the effectiveness of their campaigns. This can be difficult as there are a variety of metrics to track, such as engagement, conversions, and ROI. Additionally, it's important to remember that not all metrics are created equal, so it's crucial to understand which metrics are most important for your campaign.

To measure the effectiveness of your campaigns, CPG founders should establish clear goals and KPIs for the campaign. This can be done by identifying the metrics that align with your campaign goals, such as engagement, conversions, or ROI. Additionally, CPG founders can use tools such as Google Analytics or social media analytics to track these metrics. Finally, CPG founders can work with the influencer to track the effectiveness of the campaign by incorporating unique tracking links or promo codes into the content.

Challenges of managing relationships with influencers

A prevalent issue faced by CPG founders in the field of influencer marketing is managing relationships with influencers. From setting expectations to ensuring compliance with legal and ethical guidelines, the process can be a tricky one. However, with the right approach, it is possible to establish successful partnerships that can help drive sales and build brand awareness.

The first challenge in working with influencers is setting expectations. The influencer marketing industry is relatively new and, as a result, there is still a lot of confusion and misunderstanding around what influencers can deliver. Many influencers promise the world but don't always deliver on their promises. To avoid disappointment, it is important to be clear and transparent about what you expect from the influencer. This includes setting clear goals for the campaign, such as increasing brand awareness or driving sales. It is also important to be realistic about the influencer's reach and audience, as well as their ability to deliver on their promises.

To set the right expectations, it is important to do your research. Look at the influencer's track record and see what kind of results they have delivered for other brands. This will give you a good idea of what they are capable of and what you can expect from them. It is also important to have a clear understanding of the influencer's audience and the demographics they are targeting. This will help you identify which influencers are the best fit for your brand and your campaign.

Another important challenge when working with influencers is ensuring compliance with legal and ethical guidelines. Influencer marketing is a regulated industry, and there are strict guidelines that must be followed. For example, influencers must disclose when a post is sponsored, and they must not make false or misleading claims about a product. CPG founders must keep abreast of the latest regulations and ensure that the influencer understands and complies with them. Failure to do so can result in fines or even legal action.

To ensure compliance, it is important to have a clear and well-defined contract in place. This should include details of the campaign, the influencer's responsibilities, and the legal and ethical guidelines that must be followed. It is also important to have a system in place to monitor the influencer's activity and ensure that they are complying with the guidelines. This could include using a tool to track mentions of your brand and monitoring social media activity.

Retaining influencers for future campaigns is another important challenge when working with influencers. Influencer marketing is a rapidly-evolving industry, and influencers are always on the lookout for new and exciting opportunities. To retain influencers for future campaigns, it is important to build a strong and trusting relationship with them. This includes being responsive to their needs, being transparent about your expectations, and treating them with respect.

To retain influencers, it is important to be transparent about the results of the campaign. Share with them the metrics, the outcome and the impact that the campaign had on your business. Influencers are more likely to want to work with you again if they can see that their efforts have had a positive impact on your business. Additionally, it is important to be responsive to their needs, to be responsive to their questions, and to be responsive to their concerns. By being responsive, you will be establishing trust and mutual respect, which are the building blocks of a long-term relationship.

Key takeaways

  • Identifying influencers with a relevant audience is crucial for the success of an influencer marketing campaign.
  • Measuring the influence of an influencer and ensuring the authenticity of their audience is important before partnering with them.
  • Aligning campaign goals with the influencer's personal brand, creating content that resonates with the influencer's audience, and measuring the effectiveness of the campaign are important steps to creating effective campaigns.
  • Managing the expectations of influencers, ensuring compliance with legal and ethical guidelines, and retaining influencers for future campaigns are important for maintaining successful relationships with influencers.
